# Clinical Reference Guide

## Hypertension Management (JNC 8 Guidelines)
- Normal BP: <120/80 mmHg
- Elevated: 120-129/<80 mmHg
- Stage 1 HTN: 130-139/80-89 mmHg
- Stage 2 HTN: >=140/>=90 mmHg

### First-line Treatment
- Age <60: Target <140/90
- Age >=60: Target <150/90
- Diabetes/CKD: Target <130/80
- ACE inhibitors or ARBs preferred if proteinuria present

### Drug Selection by Comorbidity
- Heart failure: ACEi/ARB, beta-blocker, diuretic
- Post-MI: Beta-blocker, ACEi
- Diabetes: ACEi or ARB (renal protection)
- CKD: ACEi or ARB (slow progression)

## Diabetes Management (ADA Guidelines 2024)
### Diagnostic Criteria
- A1C >= 6.5%
- Fasting glucose >= 126 mg/dL
- 2-hour glucose >= 200 mg/dL (OGTT)
- Random glucose >= 200 mg/dL with symptoms

### Glycemic Targets
- A1C: <7% for most adults
- Preprandial glucose: 80-130 mg/dL
- Postprandial glucose: <180 mg/dL

### Medication Intensification
- Step 1: Metformin + lifestyle
- Step 2: Add second agent if A1C remains above target after 3 months
- Consider GLP-1 RA or SGLT2i for cardiovascular/renal benefits

## Vital Signs Reference
### Heart Rate
- Normal: 60-100 bpm
- Tachycardia: >100 bpm at rest
- Bradycardia: <60 bpm
- HR >120 sustained: requires evaluation

### Oxygen Saturation
- Normal: 95-100%
- Mild hypoxia: 90-94%
- Moderate hypoxia: 85-89%
- Severe hypoxia: <85%

### Respiratory Rate
- Normal: 12-20 breaths/min
- Tachypnea: >20 breaths/min
- Bradypnea: <12 breaths/min

## Red Flags Requiring Immediate Attention
- SpO2 <90%
- BP >180/120 (hypertensive emergency)
- HR >150 or <40 bpm
- Chest pain with autonomic symptoms
- Altered mental status
- Severe shortness of breath
