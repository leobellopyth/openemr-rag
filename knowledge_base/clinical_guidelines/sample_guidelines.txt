## Drug Interaction Example

### Warfarin and Aspirin Interaction
- Mechanism: Both drugs affect blood clotting through different mechanisms
- Warfarin: Inhibits vitamin K-dependent clotting factors
- Aspirin: Inhibits platelet aggregation
- Combined effect: Increased risk of bleeding
- Clinical consideration: Monitor INR closely if combination is necessary

### Metformin and Contrast Dye
- Context: Patients on metformin undergoing procedures requiring iodinated contrast
- Risk: Potential for lactic acidosis in patients with renal impairment
- Recommendation: Check renal function before procedure
- Timing: Hold metformin 48 hours before and after contrast administration

## Clinical Decision Support Examples

### Blood Pressure Management
- Target BP for most adults: <130/80 mmHg
- First-line agents: ACE inhibitors, ARBs, calcium channel blockers
- Monitoring: Check potassium and creatinine 1-2 weeks after initiation

### Diabetes Management
- Target HbA1c: <7% for most patients
- First-line: Metformin
- Add second agent if not at target after 3 months
- Consider patient-specific factors (age, comorbidities, hypoglycemia risk)
